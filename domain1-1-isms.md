## Why do organisations implement an ISMS?
- Protect sensitive information
- Reduce security risks
- Meet legal requirements


## CIA Triad

- Confidentiality 
   Ensure information is only accessible to authorised people and protectcted from unauthorised access. 
   Westpac and Allianz implements MFA authentication, industry standard encryption and enterprise access controls systems.

- Integrity
   Ensuring information is accurate and not altered.
   Allianz maintains the integrity of insurance claims and costumer records through audit logs, input validations, and controlled systems updates to prevent unauthorized changes.

- Availability
  Ensuring systems, applications and data are accessible when needed by authorised users.
  Westpac and Allianz uses backup systems, implements disaster recovery plans and infrastructure monitoring.

## Key Terms

Threat - anything that could exploit a weakness.
Vulnerability - A gap in security that could be exploited.
Risk - The potential loss or damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability.
Impact - The consequence of a security incident.
Control - A safeguard implemented to reduce security risks. e.g. Firewalls, encryption, security policies.
Asset - Anything that is valuable to an organisation that requires protection. e.g. data, IPs

## Threat vs Vulnerability vs Risk — How they relate
A threat is something that can cause harm such as hacker or malware. A vulnerability is a weakness that a threat can exploit, for example weak passwords or outdate softwares version. Risks exists when a threat is able to exploit a vulnerability and cause an impact to the organisation.

## PDCA Cycle

Do - The planned controls and processs are implemented.
Check - The organisation monitors, reviews and audits.
Act - Improvement are made based on audit results, incidents and reviews.

## ISO 27001 and related standards

ISO 27001 - Specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving information security mgmt systems (ISMS)
ISO 27002 - Provides guidance and best practices for selecting and implementing information security.
ISO 27003 - Provides guidance on how to implement an ISM based on the req . of ISO 27001
